Info

Carla Peters [she/her] (born 2001, Germany) studied Communication Design at Fachhochschule Aachen and École Européenne Supérieure d'Art Rennes. She currently works with the queerfeminist organization and collective lila_bunt, where she develops public relations and manages social media. She is also developing the project „Care verbindet,“ a space for young women and girls to explore themselves, connect with others, and actively counter isolation and discrimination faced by queer people in rural areas.

For her Bachelor’s project, she designed and developed the website „Display Neuke“ in collaboration with the LVR LandesMuseum Bonn, Germany. The digital platform makes the unpublished estate of German photojournalist Angela Neuke (1943-1997), held by the LVR-LandesMuseum Bonn, digitally and interactively accessible. By contextualizing the material, the analogue archive becomes a living space for knowledge exchange.

Carla’s work flows between the deeply intimate and the publicly shared. She focuses on hybrid publishing, exploring ways to create tangible and playful online spaces. Driven by a strong commitment to politically engaged design, much of her work revolves around feminist history, community care and archives.
