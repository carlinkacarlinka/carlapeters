Care Küche: Patizipative Ausstellung und Zine

Care work: stirred, shared, and politicized. We want to talk to you about what sustains us, what we are missing, and how we can help each other. Cutting, stirring, and seasoning become political acts: Who cares? Who is seen? What is missing for supportive communities?

The Care Kitchen is a participatory art practice in which care is done together. We cook spreads together, share food, and use the cooking process as a method to start conversations about care, solidarity, and queer complicity. At the same time, a collective “recipe for community care” is created, supplemented by zines to take home and reflect on. Here, care is not a private gesture, but collective work, relationship, and resistance against isolation.
